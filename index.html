Biography

I am an Assistant Professor at Jamia Millia Islamia, a Central University, New Delhi, India. Prior to joining JMI, I worked as a Research Associate at the ICAR-National Institute of Agricultural Economics and Policy Research, New Delhi, India.

I have also worked as a Research Associate at the National Academy of Agricultural Sciences (NAAS), and as a Young Professional at the Department of Commerce, Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India, New Delhi.

I hold M.Phil. and Ph.D. degrees in Economics from Aligarh Muslim University, Aligarh. My Ph.D. dissertation was titled “Performance of Sugar Mills in India: A Comparative Study of Maharashtra and Uttar Pradesh”, and my M.Phil. dissertation focused on “Efficiency of Sugar Mills in Uttar Pradesh: An Analysis”. I earned my M.A. and B.A. (Hons) in Economics along with Mathematics and Statistics from the same university.

My research interests include poverty and inequality, trade, banking, climate change and resilience, development economics, environmental and agricultural economics, marketing and value chains, sustainable agrifood systems, organic farming, water management, and agricultural policy evaluation.
